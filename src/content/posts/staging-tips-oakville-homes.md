## The Power of First Impressions

Studies consistently show that staged homes sell faster and for higher prices than unstaged ones. In a market like Oakville, where buyers have high expectations and plenty of choices, presentation can make or break a sale. Here are seven staging strategies tailored specifically for Oakville properties.

### 1. Maximize Natural Light

Oakville buyers consistently rank natural light as one of their top priorities. Remove heavy drapes and replace them with sheer panels or clean blinds. Clean every window inside and out before photography day. In rooms with limited natural light, add table lamps and floor lamps with warm-toned bulbs to create a bright, inviting atmosphere.

### 2. Declutter With the 50 Percent Rule

A good rule of thumb is to remove fifty percent of everything on surfaces, shelves, and countertops. This includes family photos, excessive decorative items, and anything that makes spaces feel smaller. The goal is to help buyers imagine their own belongings in the space.

In Oakville homes, pay special attention to the mudroom and entryway, as buyers in family-oriented neighbourhoods like River Oaks and West Oak Trails will immediately assess whether the home has practical storage for a busy household.

### 3. Invest in Curb Appeal

Oakville is a community that takes pride in its streetscapes. Before listing, invest in fresh mulch for garden beds, a power-washed driveway and walkway, seasonal planters flanking your front door, and a freshly painted front door in a classic colour like navy or charcoal.

For homes in established neighbourhoods like Old Oakville and Bronte, mature landscaping is an asset, so make sure trees and hedges are neatly trimmed rather than overgrown.

### 4. Neutralize Bold Design Choices

That accent wall you love might not appeal to buyers. Repaint any bold or unusual colours in warm neutral tones like soft white, light grey, or greige. This is one of the highest-return investments you can make before listing, often costing under two thousand dollars but adding ten times that in perceived value.

### 5. Stage the Kitchen for Lifestyle

The kitchen sells the home. Clear countertops of everything except two or three carefully chosen items: a wooden cutting board, a small herb plant, and a stylish kettle or coffee maker. Make sure all appliances are spotless and cabinet hardware is consistent and modern.

### 6. Create Defined Spaces

If you have a spare bedroom used as a catch-all storage room, stage it with a purpose: a home office, a reading nook, or a guest bedroom. Buyers need to see how every room functions. In larger Oakville homes with bonus rooms or finished basements, staging these as home theatres, play areas, or fitness rooms helps buyers envision the full lifestyle.

### 7. Appeal to the Senses

On showing days, ensure the home smells fresh and clean rather than perfumed. Avoid strong air fresheners. Instead, open windows for fifteen minutes beforehand, or simmer a pot of water with vanilla and cinnamon. Set the thermostat to a comfortable temperature, and have soft background music playing at a low volume.
